# SolidWorks Commercial Aircraft — Full Assembly

MEng Mechanical Engineering · University of Sussex  
Software: SolidWorks 2024 · SolidWorks Motion

---

Fully articulated commercial passenger aircraft modelled in SolidWorks. The assembly covers the complete airframe — fuselage, wings, tail surfaces, and fairings — alongside detailed turbofan engine sub-assemblies and a retractable landing gear mechanism. The landing gear deploys and retracts as a working mechanism, animated using SolidWorks Motion.

![Assembly Preview](preview.png)

## Overview

The aircraft was built top-down: each major component modelled as an individual part and assembled with full mate constraints. The jet engines are complete sub-assemblies — fan, nacelle, and pylon — mounted under wing. The main and nose landing gear are designed as articulated mechanisms with realistic retraction geometry, not static placeholders.

SolidWorks Motion was used to simulate and animate the full gear retraction sequence, demonstrating the kinematic validity of the mechanism design.
